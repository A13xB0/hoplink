# Come chat across MeshCore, Meshtastic, and Discord!

We've bridged our Discord server with our MeshCore and Meshtastic mesh radio
channels. Messages sent in any one of them show up in the others — no
matter which one you're on, everyone sees the same conversation.

There are two chat rooms bridged right now:

## 🟢 #scotbridge

**To try it from Discord:** just type a message in [#scotbridge](https://discord.com/channels/1525282369442021586/1525282404854534305).
It'll show up on the MeshCore mesh straight away.

**To try it from MeshCore:** join the hashtag channel `#scotbridge` in your
MeshCore app, then send a message. It'll appear in the `#scotbridge` Discord
channel under your node's name.

## 🔵 #meshtastic-general

This one connects **three** places at once — Discord, MeshCore, and
Meshtastic. A message sent from any one of them shows up on the other two.

**To try it from Discord:** type a message in [#meshtastic-general](https://discord.com/channels/1525282369442021586/1525310878537220166).
It'll go out to both the MeshCore mesh and the Meshtastic mesh.

**To try it from MeshCore:** join the hashtag channel `#longfast`, then send
a message. It'll show up in Discord and on Meshtastic.

**To try it from Meshtastic:** make sure you're on the `LongFast` channel
(the standard default public channel), then send a message. It'll show up
in Discord and on MeshCore.

## What to expect

- Your name carries across — your Discord name, MeshCore node name, or
  Meshtastic node name will show up wherever your message lands.
- Messages can take a few seconds to arrive over the mesh radio networks —
  that's just how radio works, nothing's broken.
- There's a length limit (a few hundred characters). If a Discord message is
  too long to send over the mesh, you'll get a reply back saying so instead
  of it just vanishing.
